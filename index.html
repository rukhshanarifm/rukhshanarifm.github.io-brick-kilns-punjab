Brick Kilns in Punjab, Pakistan

Rukhshan Arif Mian

Context:

Approximately 20,000 brick kilns are the primary source of brick production in Pakistan across the country's 4 provinces. Punjab is the largest in terms of population of these provinces. In 2018, the Punjab governmentâ€™s Urban Unit conducted a province-wide survey of ~6800 kilns. Information on kilns and kiln-employees was collected and published publicly.

Process:

To make bricks, soil is mixed with water and kneaded into a dough which is then placed into brick-shaped moulds. After being dried in the open, they are baked in kilns where temperatures reach 1100C. (Source: Al Jazeera).

Concerns:

Kilns are a source of air pollution and deplorable working conditions. Skin diseases, asthma and risk of tuberculosis are common amongst workers. Furthermore, employees face debt as they are forced into taking high-interest loans from kiln owners (to sustain their families). Lack of accountability and corruption result in employees being trapped by debt. Kiln employees also have a lack of access to medical facilities. Cases of child-labor are prevalent and significantly underreported.

Aim:

Our aim is to provide context to the viwer about how brick kilns are distributed across Punjab in addition to how kiln demographics may vary by districts within the province. We look at district-wise averages for age, daily wage, loan amounts and distances to educational/health facilities. Furthermore, we look at the percentages of children who earn a non-zero wage in addition to looking at the percentage of children who responded to the survey by each tehsil within a district. Tehsils can be thought of to be subdivisions of districts.
